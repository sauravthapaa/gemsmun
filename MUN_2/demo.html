Economic and Social Council

About the economic and social council

- Economic and Social Council, abbreviated ECOSOC, is one of the United Nations' six principal organs established by the UN Charter in 1945 to promote international cooperation on economic, social, cultural, educational, and health-related issues. It serves as the central forum for reflection, debate, and innovative thinking on three dimensions of sustainable development-economic, social, and environmental and is also in charge of the follow-up to major UN conferences.
